The Beginning

Portrait of an Artist

Born on September 27, 2002, in the Coachella Valley of California, Jenna Marie Ortega entered the world as the fourth of six children to Edward and Natalie Ortega. Her father, of Mexican descent, and her mother, of Mexican and Puerto Rican heritage, raised their daughter in La Quinta—a landscape of desert warmth that would contrast sharply with the dark, atmospheric roles that would later define her career.

Her journey began at age six, inspired by Dakota Fanning in Man on Fire. By nine, after relentless pleading and a monologue book gifted by her mother, a casting director discovered her online performance. What followed was a childhood spent driving six hours round-trip to Los Angeles for auditions, a sacrifice that laid the foundation for one of the most compelling careers of her generation.

From the bright innocence of Disney Channel's Stuck in the Middle to the psychological intensity of The Fallout, Ortega has demonstrated a rare ability to traverse genres. Her portrayal of Wednesday Addams in Tim Burton's Netflix series marked a cultural watershed—earning her Golden Globe, Emmy, and SAG nominations while redefining an icon for the modern age.

The Path

Chronicles of a Career

2012 — 2017

The Early Years

Debut in Rob (2012) followed by Iron Man 3 and a recurring role in Jane the Virgin. Led Disney's Stuck in the Middle as Harley Diaz, winning an Imagen Award in 2018.

2019 — 2021

The Transition

Matured into darker roles with Ellie Alves in You (Season 2). Delivered a critically acclaimed performance as Vada in The Fallout (2021), exploring the trauma of a school shooting survivor.

2022

The Scream Queen

Dominated horror cinema with Scream, X, and Studio 666. MTV Movie Award for Most Frightened Performance. Established herself as "Gen Z's Scream Queen" through fearless, committed performances.

2022 — Present

Wednesday & Nevermore

Transformed into Wednesday Addams for Tim Burton's Netflix phenomenon. Learned cello, German, and choreographed her own dance. The series amassed over 1 billion viewing hours, earning her Golden Globe, Emmy, and SAG nominations.

2024 — 2025

The Burton Continuum

Starred as Astrid Deetz in Beetlejuice Beetlejuice, opening the Venice Film Festival. Explored complex territory in Miller's Girl and led Death of a Unicorn alongside Paul Rudd. Returned as Wednesday for Season 2, now also serving as producer.
